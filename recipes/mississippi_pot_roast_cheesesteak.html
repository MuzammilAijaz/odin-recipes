Mississippi Pot Roast Cheesesteak

Description

This Mississippi Pot Roast Cheesesteak features what is arguably the best braised beef recipe ever. The slow-cooked chuck roast becomes incredibly tender and packed with rich, beefy flavor. The combination of ranch seasoning, au jus gravy mix, and pepperoncini brine creates a mouthwatering, tangy, and savory taste. If you've never tried Mississippi pot roast, prepare to be amazed—this will be one of the best sandwiches you’ll ever have!

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil
  • 4 to 5 pounds boneless beef chuck roast
  • ½ cup pepperoncini brine
  • ¼ cup water
  • 1 (1 ounce) packet ranch dressing mix (such as Hidden Valley®)
  • 1 (1 ounce) packet powdered au jus gravy mix (such as McCormick®)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, cut into slices
  • 6 pepperoncini peppers, or to taste

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Sear the chuck roast on all sides until browned, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  3. Transfer the roast to a slow cooker.
  4. Pour in the pepperoncini brine and water.
  5. Sprinkle the ranch dressing mix and au jus gravy mix evenly over the roast.
  6. Place slices of butter on top of the roast.
  7. Add whole pepperoncini peppers around the roast.
  8. Cover and cook on low for 8 hours, or until the beef is fork-tender.
  9. Shred the beef using two forks and mix it into the juices.
  10. Serve on toasted hoagie rolls with melted provolone or mozzarella cheese.
