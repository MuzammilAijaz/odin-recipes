Real N'awlins Muffuletta

Description

A muffuletta is both a type of sandwich invented by Italian immigrants in New Orleans and the type of bread it's served on.

A muffuletta consists of a halved muffuletta loaf with deli meats (usually salami, ham, and mortadella) and olive spread. The large sandwich is cut into quarters, so one loaf of bread produces at least four servings.

ingredients

  • 1 round loaf Italian bread
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 1 cup olive salad (mixed olives, giardiniera, capers, garlic, and olive oil)
  • ¼ lb (115g) Genoa salami, sliced
  • ¼ lb (115g) ham, sliced
  • ¼ lb (115g) mortadella, sliced
  • ¼ lb (115g) provolone cheese, sliced
  • ¼ lb (115g) mozzarella cheese, sliced

Steps

  1. Slice the round loaf of Italian bread in half horizontally.
  2. Brush both halves with olive oil.
  3. Spread the olive salad evenly over the bottom half of the bread.
  4. Layer the Genoa salami, ham, and mortadella on top of the olive salad.
  5. Add slices of provolone and mozzarella cheese.
  6. Place the top half of the bread back on to form the sandwich.
  7. Press down lightly to help the flavors meld.
  8. Wrap the sandwich tightly in plastic wrap and let it sit for at least 30 minutes (or refrigerate for a few hours) to enhance the flavors.
  9. Cut into quarters and serve.
